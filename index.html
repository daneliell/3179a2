Change in Gold Prices From 2000-2020

Gold has always been an important factor in world economics. Although it is no longer used as a primary form of currency in developed nations, gold still has a significant impact on a country's currency. Gold as a commodity can act as a substitute for fiat currencies and be used as an effective hedge against inflation.

Throughout the years, the price of gold of countries worldwide have been steadily rising. The chart above shows the percentage change in price of gold of ten different currencies from 2000 to 2020.

Gold Reserves Worldwide

A gold reserve is the gold held by a national central bank. It is important as it supports the value of a country's national currency.

How does it do so? The value of a country's currency is closely related to the value of its imports and exports, one of which is gold. When a importing more than exporting, a nation's currency will decline, and vice versa.

Hence, a country that has a larger gold export will see an increase in the strength of its currency when gold prices increase, since this increases the value of the country's total exports.

Similar to the price of gold, the size of gold reserves for most developed countries worldwide have been increasing from 2000 to 2015.

The charts above show the top ten largest gold reserves in the years 2000, 2005 and 2010.

The United States has consistently been one of the largest gold reserves in the world. Throughout 2005 to 2015, it has maintained its spot as the largest gold reserve and is continues to be the largest gold reserve worldwide.

European countries like Germany, France and Italy also have consistently large gold reserves throughout 2005 to 2015 behind the United States.

China's gold reserve has been steadily growing and is throughout the years and is becoming as large as other European countries.
